Growth

Next, let's see how much gaming has grown for decades.

Starting in the 80s, most of the video games are on home video game consoles. When time passes, handheld game consoles and PC games are starting to get more attention. One of the reasons might be the lag of technology. During 80s and 90s, it wasn't easy to make small gaming device and PCs weren't designed for gaming, but since the year 2000, technology are now more advanced, so the amount of video games on PCs and handheld game consoles are starting to rise significantly till today.

Sales

In the modern days, video games are known across the world. Let's see how popular of video games to each different world region.

According to the chart, you'll obviously notice that there're so many curves, up and down. This somehow related to the releases chart, but still, let's take a deeper look on some events.

  • In 1995, the first event of Electronic Entertainment Expo was held in Los Angeles Convention Center. In this event, there will be many game publishers gathering up and set up their own exhibition in the event, which could make people more interested in new video games and boost their sales up.
  • On October 2000, Sony released PlayStation 2. This boosts video game sales up in 2001-2002.
  • On November 2004, Nintendo DS was released. On November 2005, Wii and XBox360 were released. On November 2006, PlayStation 3 was released. The releases of these 4 brand new and popular gaming platforms significantly boost the video game sales up from 2004 to 2008.

By summarizing, video game sales will be greatly high for about 1-3 years before dropping down due to new console releases. This somehow decreases the sales of older platforms. Researches also show that video game sales usually rises after 1-2 years after the releases of new consoles, because developers need to study the platform before making new games.

Atari 2600

One of the oldest gaming platform.

The Atari 2600 (or Atari Video Computer System before November 1982) is a home video game console by Atari, Inc. Released on September 11, 1977, it is credited with popularizing the use of microprocessor-based hardware and ROM cartridges containing game code, a format first used with the Fairchild Channel F video game console in 1976. This format contrasts with the older model of having non-microprocessor dedicated hardware, which could only play the games that were physically built into the unit. Read more!

The PlayStation 2 (PS2) is a home video game console that was developed by Sony Computer Entertainment.
