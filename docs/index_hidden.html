Growth

Next, let's see how much gaming has grown for decades.

Starting in the 80s, most of the video games are on home video game consoles. When time passes, handheld game consoles and PC games are starting to get more attention. One of the reasons might be the lag of technology. During 80s and 90s, it wasn't easy to make small gaming device and PCs weren't designed for gaming, but since the year 2000, technology are now more advanced, so the amount of video games on PCs and handheld game consoles are starting to rise significantly till today.

Sales

In the modern days, video games are known across the world. Let's see how popular of video games to each different world region.

According to the chart, you'll obviously notice that there're so many curves, up and down. Let's take a deeper look on some events.

  • In 1995, the first event of Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3 1995) was held in Los Angeles Convention Center. In this event, there will be many game publishers gathering up and set up their own exhibition in the event. Which could make people more interested in new video games and boost their sales up.
  • On October 2000, Sony released PlayStation 2. This boosts video game sales up in 2001-2002.
  • On November 2004, Nintendo DS was released. On November 2005, Wii and XBox360 were released. On November 2006, PlayStation 3 was released. The releases of these 4 brand new and popular gaming platforms significantly boost the video game sales up from 2004 to 2008.

Atari 2600

One of the oldest gaming platform.

The Atari 2600 (or Atari Video Computer System before November 1982) is a home video game console by Atari, Inc. Released on September 11, 1977, it is credited with popularizing the use of microprocessor-based hardware and ROM cartridges containing game code, a format first used with the Fairchild Channel F video game console in 1976. This format contrasts with the older model of having non-microprocessor dedicated hardware, which could only play the games that were physically built into the unit.

  • Release Date
  • NA: 11th September 1977
  • EU: January 2017
  • JP: October 1983 (Atari 2800)
  • Introductory price: US$199 (equivalent to $786.49 in 2016)
  • Discontinued: 1st January 1992

PlayStation Portable

One of the best childhood memories.

The PlayStation Portable (PSP) is a handheld game console developed by Sony Computer Entertainment. Development of the handheld was announced during E3 2003, and it was unveiled on May 11, 2004, at a Sony press conference before E3 2004.The system was released in Japan on December 12, 2004, in North America on March 24, 2005, and in the PAL region on September 1, 2005. It primarily competed with the Nintendo DS, as part of the seventh generation of video games consoles.
